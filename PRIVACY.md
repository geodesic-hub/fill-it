# Privacy Policy — Fill It

Last updated: June 12, 2026

Fill It ("the extension") helps you autofill job-application forms from a profile
you save. This policy explains what data the extension handles and how.

## What data the extension stores

To do its job, the extension lets you save and use the following information:

- **Profile details:** full name, first/last/preferred name, email address, phone
  number, postal address (line 1/2, city, state, country, zip code), LinkedIn URL,
  website URL, and a summary/bio.
- **Work experience:** company, title, location, start/end dates, and role
  descriptions.
- **Education:** school, degree, field of study, and start/end years.
- **Preferences:** the last tab you viewed and your selected theme.

## How your data is stored and used

- All of the above is stored **locally on your device** using the browser's
  built-in extension storage (`chrome.storage`). Profile, experience, and
  education data are saved with `chrome.storage.sync`, which means Chrome may
  sync them across devices where you are signed in to the same browser account,
  under Google's control. Preferences are stored with `chrome.storage.local`.
- The data is used **only** to fill form fields on the web page you are actively
  viewing, when you click "Fill."
- The extension does **not** send your data to the developer or to any third
  party. There are no analytics, no tracking, and no external servers. No data
  ever leaves your browser/Google account.

## Data sharing

We do **not** sell, rent, transfer, or share your data with anyone. We do not use
it for advertising, creditworthiness, lending, or any purpose unrelated to
autofilling forms at your request.

## Permissions

The extension requests only the permissions needed to autofill forms: access to
save your data (`storage`), to read and fill the fields of the page you're on
(`activeTab`, `scripting`, and host access), and to show its interface in the
side panel (`sidePanel`). It does not read or collect page content beyond filling
the fields you ask it to.

## Removing your data

You can delete all stored data at any time by removing each entry in the
extension, or by uninstalling the extension from `chrome://extensions`, which
clears its local storage.
